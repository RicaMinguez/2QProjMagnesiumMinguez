Pollution: Causes and Solutions: Light Pollution
Water Pollution
Water pollution hurts all life forms that need water to drink – from humans to elephants to grass. Sometimes we can see water pollution – think of foam, formed from the build-up of toxic chemical waste, flowing down rivers, and oil spills out at sea. Sometimes, we cannot see it – think of tiny microplastics.
Causes of Water Pollution
  • Improper disposal of chemical waste from factories, mining sites, etc.
  • Excessive production of non-biodegradable trash (plastic bottles, ropes and nets, ink cartridges, etc.)
  • Oil Spills
  • Letting feces and other such waste flow straight into rivers/streams

How to Help Limit Water Pollution
  • Use reusable items (metal straws, reusable water bottles, washable metal/plastic utensils, etc.) instead of single-use items
  • Buy merchandise that uses minimal plastic packaging
  • Support groups that carry out beach clean-ups and other similar efforts. You could donate to them, help them get more publicity, or even join them in clean-up efforts when it is safe to do so.
  • If you can, avoid buying products from companies that have a recent history of irresponsibly causing water pollution.
